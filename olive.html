Olive

Problem

Patients need a way to quickly, simply and uniquely understand the different treatments they are undergoing and how much they are helping them, plus methods for daily self-care reminders and means for doing so.

Challenge

The challenge for Olive is to be an app that allows information about environmental stressors, that these results can be presented in an easy and visual way, being able to share them and the ability for self-knowledge through reading articles and exercises proposed by them.

Surveys & Interviews

A 15-20 minute interview was conducted with 3 participants with the aim of better understanding what their needs are in relation to a mental health app, determining when and how they seek help and discovering points of conflict with current methods that they use. Participants were aged 20-50 years and had different financial and demographic backgrounds

Wireframes

All wireframes went through the low-fidelity version, in which paper and pencil were used to create concepts and solutions. Next, they went through the mid-fidelity version and finally the high-fidelity version, in which UI elements were later added.

Usability Testing

Accessibility

For mine first change I compared the contrast between background and my buttons and added frames on them to increase this contrast and be previewed. By doing this, I also made it easier to understand the click area of the buttons.

For the search bar I added a label as well since the placeholders were the only ones that also served for this purpose, in order for the user to always know in which field he is typing. And I also added the possibility of dictating the search for users who need it, I added a frame and increased the contrast of the background colors with the button

Retrospect

As Olive was my first designer, working as a UX designer, I had many challenges such as learning to work with programs, collecting data, making decisions and absorbing all the knowledge provided. I made many mistakes along the way, decisions that I had to revisit, such as adding online consultations within the app, which would be impractical and not the purpose of the app at all. But including the mistakes made, I can only thank the learning process I had with this project. I would have liked to add more elements to my calendar feature and it will be added in the near future and I hope that like me, Olive continues to evolve and learn from the UX design method.
